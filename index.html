About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Political Science at UC Riverside, with research interests in American public opinionpolitical behavior. My dissertation, Consequences of and Remedies for Partisan Dehumanization in the U.S., is co-chaired by Jennifer Merolla and Daniel Biggers, with Cindy Kam, and supported by Russell Sage Foundation and Center for Ideas and Society.

My work has appeared in PS: Political Science & Politics, with a recent paper conditionally accepted at the American Political Science Review. I use survey experiments, observational data, and revealed-preference election results to study how Americans perceive their political opponents and how those perceptions shape attitudes toward democracy, redistribution, and immigration.

I expect to be on the academic job market in 2026–2027.
